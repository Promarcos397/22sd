Scrollytelling Blueprint

What Jumping Spiders Teach Us About Color

A narrative outline for building a rich, interactive web experience that blends 3D storytelling with evolutionary biology, optics, and a healthy dose of curiosity.

Frameworks: React or Svelte · Three.js · React Three Fiber · GSAP Assets: Blender models · GLTF/GLB exports · Custom shaders
Section 1 · The Yellow Illusion

You Are Not Looking at a Yellow Ball

Start with a bright yellow sphere floating in darkness. On scroll, dive through the surface until the color reveals its secrets: a lattice of red and green sub-pixels faking yellow for the human eye. The hook shows how trichromats perceive a color that does not truly exist on screen.

Diagram: schematics that show red + green stimuli creating a perceived yellow signal.

Zoom-driven transition from macro to sub-pixel grid.
Section 2 · The Fly Tiger

Meet the Jumping Spider

Introduce the protagonist with a responsive 3D model that tracks the pointer like a curious hunter. Secondary eyes offer a monochrome motion map while the principal eyes promise razor-sharp detail.

Mouse-tracking head movement on a high-quality GLTF model.
Section 3 · The Galilean Telescope

Telescopes in the Head

Shift to an X-ray view: the cephalothorax fades so the long eye tubes become visible. Explain how a large outer lens and a second lens at the tube base create a Galilean telescope with a narrow field but crisp resolution.

Diagram: side-by-side comparison of a Galilean telescope and the jumping spider eye tube anatomy.

Opacity shift reveals internal eye-tube geometry.
Section 4 · The Evolution of Red

Reinventing the Wheel

Showcase an evolutionary tree that lights up where red vision evolved independently. Describe gene duplication and red-filter hacks that convert green-sensitive cells into red detectors.

Interactive phylogeny with animated highlights for red-vision branches.
Section 5 · The Termite Experiment

The Taste of Color

Create a mini-game with two termites—one painted red and bitter, one gray and tasty. Toggling a training switch teaches color-savvy spiders to avoid red, showing the survival payoff of color vision.

Toggle training state to flip bitter/tasty cues in the UI.
Section 6 · The Depth Illusion

The Looming Effect

Visualize a tiered retina with four layers. Green focuses sharply; red overshoots and blurs, hinting at chromatic aberration. A GLSL shader can split channels to mimic defocus, turning color incapacity into a depth cue for *Mexigonus* courtship.

Diagram: chromatic aberration ray paths and blurred red focus behind the retina.

Shader-driven RGB separation to simulate defocus depth cues.
Section 7 · Conclusion

The Evolutionary Dance

Return to the yellow sphere, now filtered through "spider vision". Close with the reminder that color is a negotiation between light, biology, and perception—a dance that jumping spiders perform with extraordinary choreography.

Final morph from yellow ball to spider eye to underline the theme.
Phase Plan

Phase 1 · Assets

  • Source or model a low-poly jumping spider; isolate the head mesh.
  • Create red and gray termite textures for the mini-game.
  • Export optimized GLTF/GLB files from Blender.

Phase 2 · Hello World

  • Spin up Vite + React or Svelte.
  • Install three, @react-three/fiber, and @react-three/drei.
  • Render a test cube and confirm orbit controls.

Phase 3 · Scroll Logic

  • Pin the 3D canvas behind content; drive camera transforms with GSAP ScrollTrigger.
  • Map section entry to spider pose, opacity, and material changes.
  • Implement mouse-tracking on the principal eyes.

Phase 4 · Optics Demo

  • Add a GLSL shader to offset RGB channels for chromatic aberration.
  • Provide adjustable sliders for separation amount and focus depth.
  • Overlay diagram callouts for context.

Phase 5 · Polish

  • Apply typography and section fades to match scrollytelling mood.
  • Inline citations or timestamps for science references.
  • Optimize assets and lazy-load heavy models.
